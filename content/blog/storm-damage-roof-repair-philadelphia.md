## Types of Storms That Damage Philadelphia Roofs

The Mid-Atlantic climate throws a wide range of weather events at our homes. Understanding the types of storms that affect our region helps you know what to look for after each event.

### Nor'easters and Coastal Storms

Nor'easters are Philadelphia's signature severe weather events. These powerful coastal storms bring sustained winds of 40-60 mph with gusts exceeding 70 mph, heavy rain, and sometimes wet, heavy snow. They are particularly destructive to the flat and low-slope roofs found on South Philadelphia and Northern Liberties row homes. Wind damage roof repair requests spike after every major nor'easter, with the most common issues being lifted shingles, damaged flashing, and membrane tears on flat roofs.

### Summer Thunderstorms and Hail

From June through August, sudden severe thunderstorms can produce damaging hail, high winds, and torrential rain. Hail damage roof Philadelphia homeowners report is often deceptive -- hailstones as small as one inch in diameter can crack shingles, dent flashing, and compromise your roof's integrity without being immediately visible from the ground. Neighborhoods across Bucks County, Chester County, and Delaware County are all susceptible to these fast-moving summer storms.

### Winter Ice and Snow Events

Heavy snow loads stress flat roofs on Philadelphia row homes, while freeze-thaw cycles create ice dams along eaves. Ice dams force water underneath shingles and into your home's interior. We see this frequently in Manayunk and Roxborough, where older homes with inadequate attic insulation are particularly vulnerable.

### High Wind Events and Microbursts

Isolated high wind events and microbursts can occur during any season. These concentrated wind events can strip entire sections of shingles, topple trees onto roofs, and tear away siding and gutters. Even a brief microburst can cause damage that rivals a nor'easter in severity.

## How to Identify Storm Damage on Your Roof

After any significant storm, you need to assess your roof's condition -- but safely. Here is what to look for and how to document it.

### Exterior Signs of Damage Visible from the Ground

Before you ever climb a ladder, walk around your property and look for these indicators:

- **Missing or displaced shingles** -- bare patches or shingles on the ground
- **Cracked, curled, or buckled shingles** -- signs of wind uplift
- **Dented or damaged gutters and downspouts** -- often indicates hail
- **Granule accumulation** in gutters or at downspout exits -- a sign of hail damage roof Philadelphia residents often overlook
- **Damaged or missing flashing** around chimneys, vents, and skylights
- **Fallen tree limbs** on or near the roof
- **Dented siding, window frames, or AC units** -- confirms hail was present

### Interior Warning Signs

Sometimes the first sign of storm damage shows up inside your home:

- Water stains on ceilings or walls, especially in upper floors
- Dripping water or active leaks during or after rain
- Damp or musty smell in the attic
- Daylight visible through roof boards when you inspect the attic
- Peeling paint or bubbling wallpaper near the roofline

## What to Do Immediately After Storm Damage

The first 24-48 hours after storm damage are critical. Here is your action plan:

### Step 1: Ensure Safety First

Stay off the roof. Wet, damaged roofing materials are extremely dangerous. If there are downed power lines near your home, call PECO immediately and stay clear.

### Step 2: Document Everything

Before any cleanup or temporary repairs, photograph and video all visible damage from multiple angles. Include:

- Wide shots showing the overall roof condition
- Close-ups of specific damaged areas
- Interior damage like water stains or active leaks
- Damage to other structures (garage, shed, fence)
- A date-stamped weather report from that day

This documentation is the foundation of a successful storm damage insurance claim.

### Step 3: Make Temporary Protective Repairs

You are allowed -- and expected by your insurer -- to prevent further damage. This might include:

- Placing tarps over exposed areas
- Catching leaks with buckets
- Boarding up broken windows

Save all receipts for temporary materials. These costs are typically reimbursable through your insurance claim.

### Step 4: Contact Your Insurance Company

Call your homeowners insurance provider as soon as possible to report the damage. Most Pennsylvania policies require prompt notification. Take notes during the call: the claim number, adjuster's name, and expected timeline.

## Navigating the Storm Damage Insurance Claim Process

The insurance claim process can feel overwhelming, especially when you are dealing with an active leak or major damage. Here is how it works and how we help.

### Filing Your Claim

Contact your insurer within 24-48 hours of the storm. Provide your documentation and request an adjuster visit. Your insurer will assign a claim number and schedule an inspection, usually within one to two weeks after major storms (longer if the storm was widespread).

### The Adjuster's Inspection

The insurance adjuster will visit your property to assess the damage. **We strongly recommend having your roofing contractor present during this inspection.** Our team at Adilay Roofing regularly meets with adjusters on behalf of our clients. We ensure that all damage is identified and properly documented, including issues that adjusters sometimes miss -- like hail damage that is not visible from the ground or compromised underlayment beneath intact-looking shingles.

### Understanding Your Settlement

Your insurer will provide a damage estimate and settlement offer. Review this carefully. If the estimate seems low, your contractor can provide a detailed scope of work showing the actual cost of proper repairs. In many cases, supplemental claims are necessary to cover the full repair cost.

### When to Consider a Public Adjuster

If your claim is denied or significantly underpaid, a licensed public adjuster can negotiate with your insurer on your behalf. We can recommend reputable public adjusters who work with Philadelphia homeowners.

## How to Avoid Storm Chaser Scams in Philadelphia

After every major storm, out-of-town "storm chasers" flood Philadelphia neighborhoods with door-to-door pitches. These unlicensed or barely licensed operators prey on homeowners who are stressed and eager for quick repairs. Here is how to protect yourself:

### Red Flags to Watch For

- **Door-to-door solicitation immediately after a storm** -- Legitimate contractors do not need to chase storms for work
- **Pressure to sign contracts on the spot** -- "This deal is only good today" is a manipulation tactic
- **Requests for large upfront deposits** -- Reputable contractors require little or no money down before work begins
- **Offering to waive your deductible** -- This is insurance fraud in Pennsylvania
- **No local address or office** -- Storm chasers operate from hotel rooms and move on to the next disaster
- **Out-of-state license plates on work trucks** -- A reliable sign of a transient operation
- **Cannot provide a PA contractor license number** -- Every legitimate roofer in Pennsylvania carries a verifiable license

### How to Verify a Roofing Contractor

- Confirm their PA Home Improvement Contractor license on the state's verification portal
- Check for a permanent local business address (ours is 2020 Dreer St., Unit 101, Philadelphia, PA 19125)
- Read reviews on Google, Yelp, and the BBB
- Ask for proof of insurance -- both liability and workers' compensation
- Request local references from recent projects
- Verify they offer manufacturer-backed warranties

## Wind Damage Roof Repair: What Philadelphia Homeowners Should Know

Wind damage roof repair is among the most common storm-related services we provide. Philadelphia's position along the I-95 corridor makes it susceptible to both coastal and inland wind events. Wind damage typically manifests as:

- **Lifted or creased shingles** that have lost their seal
- **Missing shingles or entire sections** blown off the roof
- **Ridge cap damage** along the roof peak
- **Soffit and fascia separation** from the roofline
- **Damaged or detached gutters**

Even if shingles appear to be in place after a windstorm, they may have been lifted and re-settled, breaking their adhesive seal. Once that seal is compromised, the next storm -- or even moderate rain -- can cause leaks and further damage. A professional inspection after any significant wind event is essential.

## Hail Damage Roof Repair in Philadelphia

Hail damage roof Philadelphia homeowners face is often the hardest type of storm damage to detect without professional training. Hail impacts create:

- **Bruised or fractured shingles** that feel soft when pressed
- **Loss of protective granules** exposing the asphalt mat beneath
- **Cracked or shattered tile on slate roofs**
- **Dents in metal flashing, vents, and gutters**
- **Circular impact marks on flat EPDM membranes

The danger of undetected hail damage is that it dramatically shortens your roof's lifespan. A roof that should last 25-30 years may fail in 10-15 years if hail damage goes unrepaired. Insurance companies are far more likely to approve claims when a licensed contractor provides thorough documentation with close-up photographs of each impact point.
