## Spring Roof Maintenance Checklist (March - May)

Spring is the most critical inspection season for Philadelphia roof maintenance. Winter inflicts the most wear on your roof, and catching problems early prevents water damage during spring rains.

### Post-Winter Damage Assessment

- **Inspect shingles from the ground with binoculars.** Look for missing, cracked, curled, or buckled shingles. Nor'easters and freeze-thaw cycles are hard on asphalt shingles, and spring is when the damage becomes visible.
- **Check flashing around chimneys, vents, and skylights.** Winter ice expansion can break flashing seals. Compromised flashing is the number one source of roof leaks on Philadelphia row homes.
- **Look for sagging or uneven areas on flat roofs.** Ponding water from snowmelt can indicate structural settling or drainage problems on the low-slope roofs common in South Philly and Northern Liberties.
- **Examine the attic for moisture, stains, or mold.** Winter condensation and ice dam leaks often leave evidence in the attic before they show up on ceilings below.

### Gutter and Drainage System Cleanup

- **Clean all gutters and downspouts thoroughly.** Remove leaves, debris, and sediment that accumulated over winter. Clogged gutters cause fascia rot, foundation damage, and ice dams the following winter.
- **Check gutter alignment and pitch.** Gutters should slope toward downspouts with no standing water. Ice and snow weight can pull gutters away from the fascia.
- **Ensure downspouts discharge at least 4-6 feet from the foundation.** Add extensions if needed to prevent basement water intrusion.
- **Inspect gutter seams and end caps for leaks.** If your gutters are older or damaged, ask us about seamless [gutter installation](/services/gutters) that eliminates leak-prone seams entirely.

### Tree and Vegetation Management

- **Trim branches that overhang or touch the roof.** Branches scrape shingles, deposit debris, and give pests a bridge to your roof. Keep a minimum 6-foot clearance.
- **Remove moss or algae growth.** Philadelphia's humid springs encourage moss on north-facing roof slopes. Moss retains moisture and accelerates shingle deterioration.

If your spring inspection reveals any of the signs you need a new roof, schedule a professional evaluation before the busy summer season begins.

## Summer Roof Maintenance Checklist (June - August)

Philadelphia summers bring intense UV radiation, high temperatures, sudden thunderstorms, and occasional hail. Summer seasonal roofing maintenance focuses on heat management and storm preparedness.

### Heat and UV Protection

- **Check attic ventilation.** Proper ventilation (intake at soffits, exhaust at ridge) keeps attic temperatures manageable and prevents premature shingle aging. An overheated attic -- common in older Philly row homes -- can void shingle warranties.
- **Inspect for blistering or cracking on flat roof membranes.** EPDM and modified bitumen roofs on row homes are exposed to direct sun all day. Surface blisters indicate moisture trapped beneath the membrane.
- **Look for excessive granule loss in gutters.** Heat accelerates granule shedding on aging asphalt shingles. Heavy granule loss means your shingles are nearing the end of their life.

### Storm Season Preparedness

- **Secure loose shingles, flashing, and ridge caps** before thunderstorm season peaks. A shingle that is starting to lift will not survive a 60 mph gust.
- **Clear all roof drains and scuppers on flat roofs.** Summer storms dump heavy rain fast, and blocked drains cause ponding that can overwhelm a flat roof membrane.
- **Check caulk and sealant around all penetrations.** Summer heat dries out sealants. Reapply where needed to prevent leak paths.

### Post-Storm Inspections

After any significant summer thunderstorm, especially those with hail or high winds, do a ground-level walkthrough of your property. Look for dented gutters, displaced shingles, or debris on the roof. ## Fall Roof Maintenance Checklist (September - November)

Fall is your last opportunity to prepare your roof for the toughest season. This is when a thorough roof inspection checklist pays the biggest dividends.

### Pre-Winter Inspection

- **Schedule a professional roof inspection.** This is the single most important item on the fall checklist. A trained inspector can identify vulnerabilities that will fail under winter stress. At Adilay Roofing, our fall inspections cover every component of your roofing system.
- **Repair or replace any damaged shingles, flashing, or sealant.** Do not enter winter with known deficiencies. Small problems in October become emergency leaks in January.
- **Inspect and repair caulking around vent pipes, chimneys, and skylights.** These joints are the weakest points on any roof and must be sealed tight before freeze-thaw cycles begin.
- **Check for proper attic insulation.** Inadequate insulation leads to heat loss, ice dams, and higher energy bills. Philadelphia row homes with shared party walls sometimes have uneven insulation that creates hot spots.

### Gutter System Preparation

- **Perform a thorough gutter cleaning after leaves have fallen.** Wait until most deciduous trees are bare -- typically mid-to-late November in Philadelphia.
- **Install or inspect gutter guards** if your home is surrounded by trees. Gutter guards significantly reduce maintenance and prevent ice dam formation.
- **Verify all downspout connections are secure.** Loose downspouts spray water against your foundation and siding during winter rains.

### Flat Roof Specific Tasks

Philadelphia has one of the highest concentrations of flat-roof row homes in the country. If your home has a flat or low-slope roof, fall maintenance is especially critical:

- **Clear all debris from the roof surface.** Leaves and debris trap moisture against the membrane.
- **Check all seams, patches, and termination points on EPDM or modified bitumen membranes.**
- **Ensure all drains and scuppers are flowing freely.**
- **Look for ponding areas** and address drainage problems before winter snow adds weight.

## Winter Roof Maintenance Checklist (December - February)

Winter Philadelphia roof maintenance is primarily about monitoring and prevention. You should not be on your roof during winter conditions, but there is plenty you can do from the ground and inside your home.

### Ice Dam Prevention and Monitoring

- **Monitor for ice dam formation along eaves.** Icicles hanging from gutters are a warning sign. Ice dams form when heat escapes through the roof, melts snow from below, and the meltwater refreezes at the colder eave.
- **Use a roof rake to remove snow from the first 3-4 feet of roof edge.** Do this from the ground only -- never climb a ladder in winter conditions. Removing snow from the eaves prevents ice dam buildup.
- **Keep attic hatches and access doors sealed and insulated.** Attic air leaks are a leading cause of ice dams on Philadelphia homes.
- **Never use salt, calcium chloride, or chemicals on your roof.** These products damage shingles, flashing, and gutters.

### Interior Monitoring

- **Check ceilings and walls for new water stains** after every snow or ice event.
- **Monitor attic temperature and humidity.** Your attic should be close to the outside temperature -- a warm attic means inadequate insulation or ventilation.
- **Listen for dripping or running water** during and after freeze-thaw cycles.
- **Watch for condensation on attic surfaces,** which indicates a ventilation problem.

### After Major Winter Storms

Philadelphia nor'easters can dump heavy, wet snow that weighs 20+ pounds per cubic foot. If your home, especially a flat-roof row home, receives more than 12 inches of wet snow:

- **Be alert for signs of structural stress:** cracking sounds, doors that suddenly stick, visible sagging, or new cracks in interior walls.
- **Do not attempt to remove snow from a flat roof yourself.** Call a professional. The risk of personal injury and roof membrane damage is significant.
- **Document any visible damage** for potential insurance claims.

## Year-Round Preventive Roof Care Tips

Beyond seasonal tasks, these ongoing practices keep your roof in peak condition:

- **Keep a roof maintenance log.** Record inspection dates, repairs, and observations. This history is valuable for insurance claims and when you eventually sell your home.
- **Address issues immediately.** A $200 repair today prevents a $5,000 repair next year. Roofing problems never get better on their own.
- **Maintain your trees.** Mature trees in neighborhoods like Germantown, Mt. Airy, and Chestnut Hill require regular trimming to protect roofs.
- **Ensure proper attic ventilation year-round.** It prevents ice dams in winter, heat damage in summer, and moisture problems in every season.
- **Know your roof's age and warranty.** Understanding how long your roof should last helps you plan for replacement before failure.
